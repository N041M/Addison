Logo exploration · the butler

Three butlers, all geometry

Nothing literal, no faces — a butler told through his objects. Each mark is built from 2–3 primitive shapes so it survives at 16px, embosses cleanly, and draws in one breath.

A · The bow tie

The uniform, reduced to its knot. Symmetric, instantly readable, sits naturally as a "tie" between you and the machine.

B · The service bell

Ring, and Addison comes. Matches the product promise exactly: it acts when you ask, never on its own. Dome + knob + base — three shapes.

C · The monogram

An "A" wearing the bow tie as its crossbar. The most brandable: monogram, icon, and wordmark initial in one.

D · The butler himself

Head, shoulders, and a shirt-front cut from negative space, bow tie at the collar. A person, still only five shapes.
